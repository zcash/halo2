Multipoint opening argument

Consider the commitments A,B,C,D to polynomials a(X),b(X),c(X),d(X). Let's say that a and b were queried at the point x, while c and d were queried at both points x and ωx. (Here, ω is the primitive root of unity in the multiplicative subgroup over which we constructed the polynomials).

We can group the commitments in terms of the sets of points at which they were queried: ​{x}AB​​{x,ωx}CD​​

The multipoint opening optimisation proceeds as such:

  1. Sample random x3​, at which we evaluate a(X),b(X),c(X),d(X).

  2. The prover provides evaluations of each polynomial at each point of interest: a(x3​),b(x3​),c(x3​),d(x3​),c(ωx3​),d(ωx3​)

  3. Sample random x4​, to keep a,b,c,d linearly independent.

  4. Accumulate polynomials and their corresponding evaluations according to the point set at which they were queried: q_polys: q1​(X)q2​(X)​==​a(X)c(X)​++​x4​b(X)x4​d(X)​ q_eval_sets:

            [
            [a(x_3) + x_4 b(x_3)],
            [
                c(x_3) + x_4 d(x_3),
                c(\omega x_3) + x_4 d(\omega x_3)
            ]
        ]

    NB: q_eval_sets is a vector of sets of evaluations, where the outer vector goes over the point sets, and the inner vector goes over the points in each set.

  5. Interpolate each set of values in q_eval_sets: r_polys: r1​(X)s.t.r2​(X)s.t.​r1​(x3​)r2​(x3​)r2​(ωx3​)​===​a(x3​)+x4​b(x3​)c(x3​)+x4​d(x3​)c(ωx3​)+x4​d(ωx3​)​

  6. Construct f_polys which check the correctness of q_polys: f_polys f1​(X)f2​(X)​==​X−x3​q1​(X)−r1​(X)​(X−x3​)(X−ωx3​)q2​(X)−r2​(X)​​

    If q1​(x3​)=r1​(x3​), then f1​(X) should be a polynomial. If q2​(x3​)=r2​(x3​) and q2​(ωx3​)=r2​(ωx3​) then f2​(X) should be a polynomial.

  7. Sample random x5​ to keep the f_polys linearly independent.

  8. Construct f(X)=f1​(X)+x5​f2​(X).

  9. Sample random x6​, at which we evaluate f(X): f(x6​)​==​f1​(x6​)x6​−x3​q1​(x6​)−r1​(x6​)​​++​x5​f2​(x6​)x5​(x6​−x3​)(x6​−ωx3​)q2​(x6​)−r2​(x6​)​​

  10. Sample random x7​ to keep f(X) and q_polys linearly independent.

  11. Construct final_poly, final_poly(X)=f(X)+x7​q1​(X)+x72​q2​(X), which is the polynomial we commit to in the inner product argument.
